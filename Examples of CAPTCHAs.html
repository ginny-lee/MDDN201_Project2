Examples of CAPTCHAs

If you look around there are CAPTCHAs are all over the internet. CAPTCHAs have become something that you're used to that you just ignore it. Here are some examples of websites using CAPTCHAs to keep their websites safer.

Kissasian:
Kissasian is a very popular streaming website that streams asian movies and TVshows. This website also allows users to downlload movies for hgih quality. Before watching a movie or TV show the website asks the user to solve a picture identification CAPTCHA. This is for high securty but also so spam bots and attacks dont occure. As a user myself I find it a bit annoying to have to solve a CAPTCHA before everything I watch but I would much rather spend a bit of my time solving a CAPTCHA than having to think that the website may get attacked and spammed.

Zoom:
Zoom is a online video conferenceing service that people use to virtually meet with others. Users can connect by video or audio or both! In 2020 Zoom has hit great hights ecently claming that 300 million people use zoom a day. Because Zoom is used for meetings and conferences which are confidential and need high security so they added captchas into their website and on APP. This helps with blocking all the bots out and only allowing humans to join meetings. Zoom uses a wide range of CAPTCHA methods such as RECAPTCHAS as shown in the image below, word problem captchas and social media login CAPTCHAs.

Facebook:
I presume that everyone knows what Facebook is. Facebook also uses CAPTCHAs for verification. This helps identify if the user is a human or a bot and prevents facebook accounts from getting hacked. Facebook only uses the word problem CAPTCHA but recently there have been rumours going around that they are releasing a photo CAPTCHA test where they ask the user to upload a photo of their face to verify their idenitiy and to prove that they are a real person.
